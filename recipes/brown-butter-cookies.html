Brown Butter Chocolate Chip Cookies

The brown butter is non-negotiable. It's what separates the good from the legendary.

⏱ 45 min
🍽 24 cookies
🔥 Easy

Ingredients

  • 1 cup (2 sticks) unsalted butter
  • 1 cup packed brown sugar
  • ½ cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2¼ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1½ tsp salt (sounds like a lot — it's right)
  • 2 cups chocolate chips or chopped chocolate (mix of dark and semi-sweet)
  • Flaky sea salt for finishing (Maldon, ideally)

Instructions

  1. Brown the butter: Cut butter into chunks and melt in a saucepan over medium heat. Swirl occasionally. It'll foam, then the foam will subside, and you'll see golden-brown specks forming at the bottom. It'll smell nutty and toasty — that's the moment. Pull it off the heat immediately (about 5-7 minutes total). Pour into a heatproof bowl and let cool for 10 minutes.
  2. Whisk both sugars into the browned butter until combined. Add eggs one at a time, whisking after each. Stir in vanilla.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together flour, baking soda, and salt. Add to the wet mixture and fold with a spatula until just combined — a few flour streaks are fine.
  4. Fold in the chocolate chips. The dough will be soft and glossy.
  5. Chill the dough for at least 30 minutes (up to 72 hours — longer = better flavor). This is the hardest step but it matters.
  6. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Line baking sheets with parchment paper.
  7. Scoop dough into balls (~2 tbsp each) and place 3 inches apart. They spread. Sprinkle each ball with a pinch of flaky sea salt.
  8. Bake for 10-12 minutes — pull them when the edges are set but the centers still look slightly underdone. They'll firm up as they cool.
  9. Let cool on the pan for 5 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack. Or just eat them warm. No judgment.
💡 Pro tip: For extra-thick, bakery-style cookies: freeze the dough balls for 1 hour before baking. Add 1-2 minutes to bake time. The frozen center stays gooey while the outside gets crispy. Game changer.
