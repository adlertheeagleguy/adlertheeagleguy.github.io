Chicken Tikka Masala

Britain's national dish, perfected in your kitchen.

⏱ 1 hr
🍽 4 servings
🔥 Medium

Ingredients

For the Chicken

  • 1.5 lbs boneless chicken thighs, cut into chunks
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 2 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp chili powder (or paprika for mild)
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch ginger, grated
  • Salt to taste

For the Sauce

  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 1 large onion, finely diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp fresh ginger, grated
  • 2 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp coriander
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • ¼ tsp cayenne (adjust to taste)
  • 1 can (15 oz) tomato sauce
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions

  1. Marinate the chicken: Mix yogurt, lemon juice, all spices, garlic, and ginger in a bowl. Add chicken and toss to coat. Cover and refrigerate at least 30 minutes (overnight is even better).
  2. Cook the chicken: Heat a skillet over high heat with a drizzle of oil. Cook marinated chicken in batches — don't crowd the pan — until charred on the outside, about 3-4 minutes per side. It doesn't need to be cooked through; it'll finish in the sauce. Set aside.
  3. Start the sauce: In a large pot or Dutch oven, melt butter over medium heat. Add onions and cook until soft and golden, about 8 minutes.
  4. Add garlic and ginger, cook 1 minute until fragrant. Add all the sauce spices (garam masala, cumin, coriander, paprika, cayenne) and stir for 30 seconds — just until they bloom and smell amazing.
  5. Pour in the tomato sauce and sugar. Simmer for 15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens and deepens in color.
  6. Stir in the heavy cream. Add the chicken and any accumulated juices. Simmer on low for 10 minutes until chicken is cooked through and the sauce is rich and velvety.
  7. Taste and adjust salt, heat, and sugar. Garnish generously with cilantro. Serve over basmati rice with warm naan.
💡 Pro tip: Broiling the marinated chicken instead of pan-searing gives you even better char. Put it on a sheet pan under the broiler for 5-6 minutes per side, watching carefully.
