Korean Popcorn Chicken

Crispy, saucy, sweet-spicy perfection. The kind of thing you crave at 2am.

⏱ 30 min
🍽 4 servings
🔥 Easy

Ingredients

Chicken

  • 2 lb chicken breast (or thighs), cut into bite-size pieces
  • 2 eggs
  • 3 tbsp soy sauce
  • ¾ tsp salt
  • ¾ tsp black pepper
  • 1½ tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp ginger powder

Crispy Coating

  • 1 cup cornstarch
  • 3 tbsp flour
  • ¾ tsp baking powder

Korean Sauce

  • ⅓ cup gochujang
  • 3 tbsp brown sugar
  • 3 tbsp ketchup
  • 4 tbsp honey
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 tbsp soy sauce
  • 3 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 1½ tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 tbsp butter

Garnish

  • Green onions, sliced
  • Sesame seeds

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, combine the chicken, eggs, soy sauce, salt, pepper, garlic powder, and ginger powder. Mix until the chicken is fully coated.
  2. Add the cornstarch, flour, and baking powder. Toss until the chicken is evenly coated.
  3. Air fryer: Preheat to 400°F. Cook for , flip the chicken, then cook another until crispy.
    Oven: Bake at 425°F for , flipping halfway through.
  4. In a pan over medium heat, combine the gochujang, brown sugar, ketchup, honey, garlic, soy sauce, rice vinegar, and sesame oil. Let it simmer for until thick, then turn off the heat and whisk in the butter.
  5. Add the crispy chicken to a bowl and pour in about ½ cup of the sauce. Toss until fully coated, adding more sauce if needed.
  6. Serve over rice and top with green onions and sesame seeds. Try not to eat it all before it hits the plate.
💡 Pro tip: Don't drown the chicken in sauce — toss it lightly so the crispy coating stays crunchy. Serve extra sauce on the side for dipping. And thighs > breasts if you want maximum juiciness.
🔥 Gochujang: If you've never used it, gochujang is Korean fermented chili paste. You can find it at most grocery stores in the international aisle, or grab it at any Asian market. It's the backbone of this entire dish — don't skip it.
