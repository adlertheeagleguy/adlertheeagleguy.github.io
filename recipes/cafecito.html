Cafecito

Cuban coffee — strong, sweet, and meant to be shared.

⏱ 15 min
🍽 4 servings
🔥 Easy

Adapted from Café Bustelo

Ingredients

  • Water (per moka pot instructions)
  • Café Bustelo espresso ground coffee (or any dark espresso grind)
  • 4 tbsp sugar (adjust to taste — this is supposed to be sweet)

Instructions

  1. Brew the espresso: Fill a stovetop moka pot with water and coffee according to manufacturer's instructions. Reassemble and start brewing over medium heat.
  2. Prep the sugar: Place in a 2-cup measuring cup or metal pitcher. As soon as espresso starts to brew, pour about ½ tablespoon over the sugar. Return moka pot to heat until done.
  3. Make the espumita: Beat the sugar and espresso with a small spoon until it turns into a creamy, pale beige paste and most of the sugar dissolves — about . This is the magic step.
  4. Pour the brewed espresso gently over the creamed sugar. Stir gently. A thin layer of foam (the espumita) should float on top.
  5. Pour into tiny espresso cups and serve immediately. ¡Salud!
💡 Pro tip: The espumita (creamy sugar foam) is what makes this cafecito and not just sweet espresso. Beat vigorously — you want the sugar to nearly dissolve into a thick paste before you pour the rest of the espresso. If you skip this, you're just having coffee with sugar.
