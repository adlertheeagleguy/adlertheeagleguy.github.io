🥣 Dough Making Instructions:

  1. Cool water to <60°F.
  2. Mix the water and yeast.
  3. Add flour and Olive Oil and mix for 2 minutes.
  4. Keep mixing on low, then add Sugar and Salt.
  5. Mix for 10 more minutes.
  6. Cover the dough with plastic wrap and rest for 1-3 hours.
  7. Divide the dough into portions.
  8. Shape into balls, sealing the seam.
  9. Put the dough in a lightly oiled container.
  10. Cover tightly and refrigerate for 2–4 days. 3 days is ideal.
  11. Let the dough come to room temperature before using.

🍅 Simple New York-Style Pizza Tomato Sauce

Ingredients (for 1 can, enough for 2–4 pizzas):

  • 1 can crushed/canned tomatoes
  • 4 grams salt

Instructions:

  1. Add about 4 grams of salt directly to the can of crushed tomatoes.
  2. Blend right in the can using an immersion blender (or transfer to a regular blender).
  3. Blend just enough to smooth it out—don't over-blend.
  4. That's it—super simple sauce ready to use (about ½ cup per pizza).

🧀 New York-Style Cheese Blend for Pizza

Ingredients (for 1 pizza):

  • 8 ounces low-moisture mozzarella (50/50 blend: half whole-milk + half part-skim)

Instructions:

  1. Shred your own low-moisture mozzarella block (use a 50/50 mix of whole-milk and part-skim for the best taste without getting too greasy).
  2. Mix the shredded mozzarella well and set aside.
  3. Use the full 8 ounces when topping the pizza.

🍕 New York-Style Pizza

Important Equipment: Pizza steel (highly recommended), Pizza peel, Oven that can reach its highest temperature (ideally 500–550°F / 260–288°C)
Timing Notes: Take dough out 3–4 hours before baking to reach room temperature. Preheat oven (with pizza steel inside) to its highest temperature for about 1 hour.

Ingredients (per pizza):

  • Room-temperature New York-style pizza dough
  • Sauce: 1 can (28oz) crushed/canned tomatoes, About 4 grams salt per can
  • Cheese: 8 ounces low-moisture mozzarella (50/50 blend: half whole milk + half part-skim)
  • Flour (for dusting)
  • Semolina flour (for dusting peel / preventing sticking)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven: Place your pizza steel on the middle or top rack. Preheat the oven to its highest temperature setting (usually 500–550°F) and let it heat for a full hour so the steel gets very hot.
  2. Set up your work area: Lightly flour your work surface with all-purpose flour. Have extra flour and semolina flour nearby. Dust your pizza peel generously with a mixture of flour and semolina flour (this prevents sticking when launching).
  3. Shape the dough: Place the room-temperature dough ball onto the floured surface. Dust the top lightly with flour. Gently press out large air bubbles with your fingertips. Begin pressing and stretching the dough into a circle, making it slightly larger than your hand span.
  4. Transfer to peel: Once shaped, carefully lift the dough onto your prepared, generously dusted pizza peel. Give it a gentle shake to confirm it slides easily. If it sticks, add more flour/semolina underneath.
  5. Top the pizza: Spread approx ½ cup of tomato sauce in a thin, even layer. Add the 8 ounces of shredded low-moisture mozzarella blend evenly. Add whatever toppings you want.
  6. Final stretch (optional): If you want a slightly larger or thinner pizza, give the dough one last gentle stretch from the edges while it's on the peel.
  7. Launch the pizza: Open the oven and carefully angle the peel toward the back of the steel. Give the peel a quick back-and-forth shake so the pizza slides off onto the hot steel.
  8. Bake: Bake for 7–8 minutes at the oven's maximum temperature (around 550°F if possible). Rotate halfway if your oven has hot spots. The crust should be puffed, lightly charred in spots, and the cheese bubbly and golden.
  9. Finish and serve: Remove from the oven when done. Let rest 1–2 minutes, slice, and serve hot.
